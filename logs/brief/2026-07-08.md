## Shopify

- Manual payment methods (wire, cash, bank transfer) now display in the buyer's local currency at checkout and on order confirmations, pulling from Shopify Markets config. B2B merchants who rely on wire payment terms have always had to choose between manual payment options and correct currency display across markets. That gap is closed. (https://changelog.shopify.com/posts/manual-payment-methods-now-support-multi-currency-presentment)
- Four new granular staff permissions live in admin: manage payment settings, manage disputes, view payouts, view tax documents. Plus merchants running finance teams can now delegate payment access without handing over full account rights. (https://changelog.shopify.com/posts/new-and-updated-staff-permissions-for-payments-payouts-disputes-and-tax-documents)
- Shopify Flow got two fixes that eliminate the main reasons payment automations break silently: action extensions no longer fail when payload fields don't match the deployed config, and runtime URLs now resolve at execution time so workflows survive URL changes without manual reactivation. (https://shopify.dev/changelog/shopify-flow-changes-to-action-extensions-result-in-fewer-breaking-changes / https://shopify.dev/changelog/shopify-flow-action-runtime-urls-now-update-automatically)

💡 The 2026-07 draft order deposit API (Plus-only, released July 1) and today's manual-payment multi-currency fix are two halves of a B2B deposit workflow nobody has wired together yet. TermStack could close that loop: read a company's payment terms, set the deposit percentage on the draft order via Admin GraphQL, and surface the balance-due amount in the buyer's market currency at checkout — no custom checkout extensions required.
