# Brief — Require Prepayment or Deposits from First-Time B2B Buyers on Shopify

**Funnel:** BOFU — shipped feature
**Task:** cb-20260718-require-prepayment-first-time-b2b-buyers

## Primary query
- "shopify b2b first order prepayment"
- "require deposit new wholesale customer shopify"

## Direct answer (first 2 sentences — this is what AI engines quote)
Shopify can't natively distinguish a company's first order from its fiftieth — terms and deposits are static per location. A Payment Customization Function can detect first-time orders at checkout and require full or partial payment upfront, then relax terms automatically from order two.

## Outline
- The credit problem: extending Net 30 to an unproven buyer = free credit risk; running credit checks on everyone = friction (link credit-checks post)
- The standard wholesale policy: first order prepaid / 50% deposit, terms after — and why nobody enforces it consistently by hand
- The rule: first-time order condition → deposit outcome; order 2+ → tier rule takes over. Show both rules + priority
- Edge cases: what counts as "first" (per company vs per location), reorders after long gaps
- 5-minute setup + simulator preview
